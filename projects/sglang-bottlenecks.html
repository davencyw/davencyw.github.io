Uncovering and Resolving Bottlenecks in SGLang

David Blum
March, 2026

Abstract

SGLang is a high-performance inference engine for large language models, but like any complex system, it harbors non-obvious bottlenecks that emerge under real-world workloads. This project documents a systematic investigation into SGLang's performance characteristics — from profiling the runtime to identifying hot paths in the scheduler, memory management, and CUDA kernel dispatch — and the targeted optimizations applied to resolve them. The goal is to bridge the gap between theoretical hardware utilization and observed throughput in production serving scenarios.


1. Motivation

As LLM inference moves from research settings into production, inference engines like SGLang become critical infrastructure. Small inefficiencies — a suboptimal kernel selection, unnecessary CPU-GPU synchronization, or cache thrashing under bursty request patterns — can compound into significant throughput losses at scale. Rather than accepting these as fixed costs, this project takes a measurement-first approach: profile first, optimize second, and validate with reproducible benchmarks.
